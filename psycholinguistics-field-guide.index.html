A Field Guide · Psycholinguistics

The science of language in the mind

Psycholinguistics is the study of how language lives in the mind. It sits at the intersection of linguistics, cognitive psychology, and neuroscience, and it asks deceptively simple questions: how do we understand a sentence the moment we hear it, before it is even finished? How do we retrieve the right word from a mental vocabulary of tens of thousands in under a second? How does a child acquire an entire grammatical system in just a few years, with no formal instruction?

The answers turn out to be surprisingly complex and revealing. Research on memory and sentence processing shows that the brain is constantly making predictions about what comes next, loading upcoming words into a kind of mental buffer before they arrive. Studies of bilingual speakers show that both languages stay active in the mind simultaneously, each one quietly competing with the other even when only one is being used. When a stroke damages a specific region of the brain, it can selectively strip away a person's ability to form grammatical sentences while leaving their vocabulary intact, or vice versa, offering a rare glimpse into how language is organised at the neural level. And the recent emergence of large language models has thrown a new kind of question into the mix: if a machine trained purely on text can predict language with human-like fluency, what does that tell us about how humans actually acquire and process it?

This resource is built for students and curious minds who want to find their footing in the field — whether you have just encountered psycholinguistics for the first time, are considering it as a research direction, or simply want to know where the interesting work is happening. Here you will find the key journals where research is published, the institutions leading the field globally, the major research questions driving current work, and the conferences where the community gathers.

Research Focuses

The major streams of psycholinguistic inquiry over the past two decades, each with its own methods, debates, and real-world implications. Many of these streams increasingly intersect with AI and computational language modelling.

Sentence Processing

Language Prediction & Anticipation

How do listeners and readers predict upcoming words before they arrive? Research using eye-tracking, EEG, and reading time paradigms has shown that the brain is continuously generating predictions about what comes next — making language comprehension an active, anticipatory process.

Key questions
  • What contextual cues drive prediction?
  • Do good predictors process language more efficiently?
  • How does prediction interact with working memory?
Eye-tracking ERP Reading
Sample Papers
  • What do we mean by prediction in language comprehension? Kuperberg & Jaeger · Psychonomic Bulletin & Review · 2016
  • Integration and Prediction in Language Processing: A Synthesis Ferreira & Chantavarin · Current Directions in Psychological Science · 2018
  • Incremental interpretation at verbs: Restricting the domain of subsequent reference Altmann & Kamide · Cognition · 1999 — the classic VWP prediction study
Bilingualism

Bilingual Language Processing

How do bilinguals manage two languages in a single cognitive system? Research explores how both languages are simultaneously active, how speakers switch between them, and what this reveals about cognitive control and executive function more broadly.

Key questions
  • How is language selection controlled?
  • Does bilingualism confer cognitive advantages?
  • How do the two languages interact during comprehension?
Code-switching Executive Function Cognitive Control
Sample Papers
  • Bilingualism reveals fundamental variation in language processing Fricke, Zirnstein, Navarro-Torres & Kroll · Bilingualism: Language and Cognition · 2019
  • The Revised Hierarchical Model: A critical review and assessment Kroll, Van Hell, Tokowicz & Green · Bilingualism: Language & Cognition · 2010
  • The Advantages of Bilingualism Debate Antoniou · Annual Review of Linguistics · 2019
Acquisition

First & Second Language Acquisition

How do children acquire their first language with such remarkable speed and accuracy? And how does adult second language learning differ? This stream spans developmental psychology, formal linguistics, and educational research.

Key questions
  • What is the role of statistical learning in acquisition?
  • Is there a critical period for language?
  • How does L1 structure influence L2 processing?
Developmental SLA Input & Exposure
Sample Papers
  • Statistical Learning by 8-Month-Old Infants Saffran, Aslin & Newport · Science · 1996 — foundational statistical learning paper, 4,900+ citations
  • A critical period for second language acquisition: Evidence from 2/3 million English speakers Hartshorne, Tenenbaum & Pinker · Cognition · 2018 — large-scale reappraisal of the critical period hypothesis
  • Studying SLA in the age of large language models Marian · Brain and Language · 2023 — emergentist accounts of SLA and LLM parallels
Neuroscience

Neural Bases of Language

What happens in the brain when we process language? Neuroimaging and EEG studies have refined our understanding of Broca's and Wernicke's areas, and identified distributed networks involved in syntax, semantics, and prosody.

Key questions
  • Are syntactic and semantic processing neurally distinct?
  • How does the brain recover language function after stroke?
  • What do language disorders reveal about normal processing?
fMRI EEG Aphasia
Sample Papers
  • The cortical organization of speech processing Hickok & Poeppel · Nature Reviews Neuroscience · 2007 — the dual-stream model, 5,000+ citations
  • The neural architecture of language: Integrative modeling converges on predictive processing Schrimpf et al. · PNAS · 2021 — benchmark linking LLMs, fMRI, and behavioural data
  • The Brain Basis of Language Processing: From Structure to Function Friederici · Physiological Reviews · 2011 — comprehensive neuroimaging review across fMRI, EEG, MEG
Speech

Speech Perception & Production

How do listeners map continuous acoustic signals onto discrete linguistic units? And how does the speaker plan and execute utterances in real time? This stream is closely connected to phonetics, audiology, and AI speech recognition.

Key questions
  • How is variability in speech normalised by listeners?
  • What drives speech errors and self-monitoring?
  • How do humans outperform machines in noisy conditions?
Phonetics ASR Prosody
Sample Papers
  • Tracking the time course of spoken word recognition using eye movements Allopenna, Magnuson & Tanenhaus · Journal of Memory and Language · 1998
  • Four central questions about prediction in language processing Huettig · Brain Research · 2015
  • Predictive language processing: integrating comprehension and production Gastaldon et al. · Frontiers in Psychology · 2024
Computation

Computational Psycholinguistics

Can computational models simulate how humans process language? This stream builds formal models of sentence processing, word recognition, and language production — increasingly in dialogue with large language models and AI.

Key questions
  • Do LLMs process language like humans do?
  • Can surprisal models predict reading times?
  • What do AI systems tell us about human cognition?
LLMs Surprisal Modelling
Sample Papers
  • Demystifying large language models in second language development research Yan Cong · Computer Speech & Language · 2024 — operationalises LLM-Surprisal for L2 writing assessment and Chinese learners' English development
  • Syntactic Structure from Deep Learning Linzen & Baroni · Annual Review of Linguistics · 2021
  • Large Language Models Are Human-Like Internally Kuribayashi et al. · Transactions of the ACL · 2025 — mechanistic interpretability links LLM layers to N400 and reading times
Applied

Language Disorders & Clinical Psycholinguistics

How do acquired and developmental language disorders — aphasia, dyslexia, SLI — inform our models of normal language processing? This clinically oriented stream connects psycholinguistics to speech therapy, education, and rehabilitation.

Key questions
  • What do breakdown patterns reveal about language architecture?
  • How can psycholinguistic theory improve intervention?
  • How does dyslexia relate to phonological processing?
Aphasia Dyslexia Intervention
Sample Papers
  • Linguistic theory and aphasia: an overview Garraffa & Fyndanis · Aphasiology · 2020 — integrates theoretical linguistics, psycholinguistics and neuroscience of language
  • A Bibliometric and Visualized Study of the Knowledge Domain of Language Disorder Research Hu, Fan & Wang · SAGE Open · 2024 — maps dominant themes across dyslexia, SLI/DLD, and aphasia research
  • Understanding Dyslexia in the Context of Developmental Language Disorders Adlof & Hogan · Language, Speech, and Hearing Services in Schools · 2018 — connects phonological deficit theory to DLD and clinical intervention
